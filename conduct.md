    <section id="conduct-purpose" class="policy-card">
      <h2>1. Purpose</h2>
      <p>
        The Malta Microsoft AI User Group (MMAUG) is committed to fostering a safe, inclusive, and collaborative
        environment for all members, regardless of experience level, background, race or identity.
      </p>
      <p>This Code of Conduct defines the expected standards of behavior for all participants across:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Community events, physical and virtual.</li>
        <li>Online platforms such as Meetup, GitHub, LinkedIn, WhatsApp, and related community spaces.</li>
        <li>Contributions to discussions, content, and shared resources.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="core-principles" class="policy-card">
      <h2>2. Our Core Principles</h2>
      <h3>2.1 Respect and Inclusivity</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Treat all participants with respect, dignity, and professionalism.</li>
        <li>Embrace diversity in background, skill level, and perspectives.</li>
        <li>Always use inclusive and appropriate language.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>2.2 Collaboration and Knowledge Sharing</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Promote open knowledge exchange.</li>
        <li>Support beginners and encourage learning.</li>
        <li>Provide constructive feedback without hostility.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>2.3 Integrity and Professionalism</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Share accurate and truthful information.</li>
        <li>Avoid misleading claims or unverified technical assertions.</li>
        <li>Respect intellectual property and give proper credit.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>2.4 Responsible AI and Technology Use</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Promote ethical use of AI systems, cloud technologies, and automation tools.</li>
        <li>Avoid discussions or demonstrations that promote harmful AI or security use without context or authorization.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="expected-behavior" class="policy-card">
      <h2>3. Expected Behavior</h2>
      <p>Participants are expected to:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Be respectful and courteous in all interactions.</li>
        <li>Listen actively and allow others to contribute.</li>
        <li>Engage in constructive discussions, even when opinions differ.</li>
        <li>Provide helpful, evidence-based contributions.</li>
        <li>Follow event guidelines and moderator instructions.</li>
        <li>Represent the community positively in public and professional spaces.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="unacceptable-behavior" class="policy-card">
      <h2>4. Unacceptable Behavior</h2>
      <p>The following behaviors are strictly prohibited.</p>

      <h3>4.1 Harassment and Abuse</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Verbal abuse, intimidation, or threats.</li>
        <li>Discriminatory language or behavior based on race, gender, religion, nationality, disability, or protected characteristics.</li>
        <li>Personal attacks or sustained disruption of discussions.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>4.2 Inappropriate Conduct</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Sexual language, imagery, or advances.</li>
        <li>Offensive jokes, slurs, or derogatory remarks.</li>
        <li>Sharing inappropriate or explicit content.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>4.3 Disruptive Behavior</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Interrupting speakers or sessions repeatedly.</li>
        <li>Trolling, spamming, or derailing discussions.</li>
        <li>Excessive self-promotion without relevance.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>4.4 Misuse of Community Platform</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Sharing confidential or proprietary information.</li>
        <li>Promoting illegal activities or unethical practices.</li>
        <li>Unauthorized recording or distribution of event content.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>4.5 Unapproved Advertising and Solicitation</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Commercial advertising or solicitation is not permitted without prior explicit approval.</li>
        <li>This includes paid training, courses, services, job postings, recruitment, and commercial offerings.</li>
        <li>This applies across all community spaces, including events, chats, comments, issues, PRs, and direct messages initiated via the community.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="speaker-responsibilities" class="policy-card">
      <h2>5. Speaker and Contributor Responsibilities</h2>
      <p>Speakers, panelists, and contributors must:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Share content that is accurate, relevant, and appropriate.</li>
        <li>Avoid disclosing confidential organizational information or client/employer-sensitive data.</li>
        <li>Clearly state when expressing personal opinions versus factual information.</li>
        <li>Acknowledge when unsure and avoid speculation.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="reporting-violations" class="policy-card">
      <h2>6. Reporting Violations</h2>
      <p>
        If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the MMAUG
        leadership team via Meetup direct message, community leadership contacts, or official communication channels.
      </p>
      <p>Reports will be treated with confidentiality, serious consideration, and prompt response.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="enforcement" class="policy-card">
      <h2>7. Enforcement and Consequences</h2>
      <p>Failure to comply with this Code of Conduct may result in:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Verbal or written warning.</li>
        <li>Removal from an event.</li>
        <li>Temporary suspension from community platforms.</li>
        <li>Permanent ban from the community.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>The leadership team reserves the right to take action necessary to maintain a safe and respectful environment.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="alignment" class="policy-card">
      <h2>8. Alignment with Community Governance</h2>
      <p>This Code of Conduct operates in conjunction with:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>MMAUG Community Governance and Operational Process.</li>
        <li>Leadership policies and participation guidelines.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>Violations may also impact leadership eligibility, volunteer status, and recommendation eligibility.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="safe-community" class="policy-card">
      <h2>9. Commitment to a Safe Community</h2>
      <p>By participating in MMAUG, you agree to:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Uphold this Code of Conduct.</li>
        <li>Contribute to a positive, inclusive, and professional environment.</li>
        <li>Support the mission of advancing AI, cloud, and DevOps knowledge responsibly.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="disclaimer" class="policy-card">
      <h2>10. Disclaimer</h2>
      <p>
        All opinions expressed during MMAUG events and discussions are those of the individual speakers or participants
        and are not representative of their employers or affiliated organizations.
      </p>
      <p>
        Participants are encouraged to speak truthfully and responsibly, avoid sharing unverifiable or sensitive
        information, and indicate when they will follow up on uncertain responses.
      </p>
    </section>

    <section id="acknowledgment" class="policy-card">
      <h2>11. Acknowledgment</h2>
      <p>Participation in the Malta Microsoft AI User Group constitutes acceptance of this Code of Conduct.</p>
    </section>
