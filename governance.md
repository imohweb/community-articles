    <section id="purpose" class="policy-card">
      <h2>1. Purpose of the Community</h2>
      <p>
        The Malta Microsoft AI User Group (MMAUG) is a technology community dedicated to advancing knowledge,
        collaboration, and practical skills in Microsoft AI technologies, cloud infrastructure, DevOps, intelligent
        systems, and open-source technologies.
      </p>
      <p>The community exists to:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Promote open knowledge sharing around Microsoft AI technologies and open-source technologies.</li>
        <li>Provide free and accessible learning opportunities for developers, engineers, architects, and students.</li>
        <li>Build a strong AI technical ecosystem within Malta.</li>
        <li>Support the development of future AI engineers and cloud professionals.</li>
        <li>Connect members with industry experts, practitioners, and global communities.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="ai-scope" class="policy-card">
      <h2>1.1 Community Context and AI Scope</h2>
      <ul>
        <li>Educational AI usage.</li>
        <li>Demonstrations.</li>
        <li>Research discussions.</li>
      </ul>
      <p class="policy-note">Note: The community does not deploy AI systems into real-world decision-making environments.</p>
    </section>

    <section id="governance-structure" class="policy-card">
      <h2>2. Community Governance Structure</h2>
      <p>
        The community operates through a structured leadership model to ensure continuity, transparency, and effective
        operations.
      </p>
    </section>

    <section id="leadership-roles" class="policy-card">
      <h2>2.1 Leadership Roles</h2>
      <p>The MMAUG leadership structure includes:</p>

      <h3>Community Lead</h3>
      <p>The Community Lead provides strategic direction and oversight for the entire community.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Defining the strategic direction of the community.</li>
        <li>Representing the community in partnerships and external engagements.</li>
        <li>Coordinating with Microsoft and other ecosystem partners.</li>
        <li>Overseeing community programs and major events.</li>
        <li>Ensuring alignment with the community mission.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Co-Leads / Organizers</h3>
      <p>Co-leads assist with the operational execution of community activities.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Coordinating meetups and technical events.</li>
        <li>Managing speaker engagements.</li>
        <li>Supporting member onboarding and engagement.</li>
        <li>Assisting in community operations.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Technical Program Lead</h3>
      <p>Responsible for maintaining the quality and relevance of technical sessions.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Reviewing Call for Speaker submissions.</li>
        <li>Curating technical topics aligned with the Microsoft ecosystem and open-source technologies.</li>
        <li>Ensuring meetup sessions and technical articles remain high quality and relevant.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>Community Operations Lead</h3>
      <p>Responsible for logistics and operational coordination.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Managing event logistics.</li>
        <li>Coordinating volunteer activities.</li>
        <li>Managing community communication platforms such as social media, email, and newsletters.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="tenure-policy" class="policy-card">
      <h2>3. Leadership Tenure Policy</h2>
      <p>
        To encourage fresh ideas, leadership development, and community sustainability, MMAUG implements a rotational
        fixed-term leadership tenure system.
      </p>

      <h3>3.1 Community Lead Tenure</h3>
      <p>The Community Lead serves a tenure of six (6) months.</p>
      <p>At the end of the tenure period:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>The leadership team conducts a performance and impact review.</li>
        <li>The current Community Lead may step down voluntarily if they are facing personal or professional constraints.</li>
        <li>The current Community Lead may be reappointed for another tenure if they indicate interest.</li>
        <li>The current Community Lead may transition into an advisory role.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>3.2 Co-Lead and Organizer Tenure</h3>
      <p>Co-Leads and Organizers typically serve a 12-month tenure, subject to periodic review.</p>
      <p>Their continuation in leadership roles depends on:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Level of participation.</li>
        <li>Contribution to events.</li>
        <li>Community engagement impact.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>3.3 Leadership Rotation</h3>
      <p>Leadership rotation ensures:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Leadership opportunities for active members.</li>
        <li>Diversity of ideas.</li>
        <li>Community sustainability.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="removal-conditions" class="policy-card">
      <h2>3.4 Leadership Removal Conditions</h2>
      <p>
        To maintain accountability, professionalism, and operational efficiency within the Malta Microsoft AI User Group
        (MMAUG), leadership roles are subject to defined removal conditions.
      </p>
      <p>A member of the leadership team may be removed from their role if any of the following conditions are met.</p>

      <h3>3.4.1 Non-Participation in Leadership Activities</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Failure to actively contribute to community operations, events, or assigned responsibilities over a sustained period.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>3.4.2 Absence from Leadership Meetings</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Failure to attend three (3) consecutive leadership meetings without prior notice or valid justification.</li>
        <li>Repeated absence with minimal engagement, even if not consecutive, may also trigger a review.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>3.4.3 Breach of Code of Conduct</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>
          Any violation of the community's Code of Conduct, including disrespectful or unprofessional behavior,
          harassment or discrimination, or actions that damage the reputation of the community.
        </li>
      </ul>

      <h3>3.4.4 Misrepresentation of the Community</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Acting on behalf of the community without authorization.</li>
        <li>Misuse of the community name, platform, or influence for personal or commercial gain without approval.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>3.4.5 Failure to Fulfill Assigned Responsibilities</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Consistent inability or unwillingness to execute agreed leadership duties.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>3.4.6 Removal Process</h3>
      <p>The removal process follows a structured and fair approach.</p>
      <ol>
        <li><strong>Review:</strong> The leadership team reviews the situation and documents the concern.</li>
        <li><strong>Notification:</strong> The affected leader is formally notified and given an opportunity to respond.</li>
        <li><strong>Decision:</strong> A decision is made based on severity, impact, and the individual's response.</li>
        <li><strong>Transition:</strong> If removal is confirmed, responsibilities are removed and a replacement may be appointed.</li>
      </ol>

      <h3>3.4.7 Reinstatement</h3>
      <p>In some cases, a removed leader may be reconsidered for future leadership roles if:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>The issues have been resolved.</li>
        <li>There is demonstrated renewed commitment.</li>
        <li>The leadership team approves re-engagement.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="volunteer-program" class="policy-card">
      <h2>4. Volunteer Program</h2>
      <p>Volunteers play an essential role in the success of the community.</p>
      <p>Typical volunteer responsibilities include:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Event coordination support.</li>
        <li>Speaker assistance.</li>
        <li>Community outreach.</li>
        <li>Social media promotion.</li>
        <li>Technical session moderation.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>Volunteers are expected to demonstrate:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Professional conduct.</li>
        <li>Reliability.</li>
        <li>Active community participation.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="screening-process" class="policy-card">
      <h2>5. Screening Process for Leadership Promotion</h2>
      <p>
        Active volunteers and community members may be considered for promotion to leadership roles through a structured
        evaluation process.
      </p>

      <h3 id="eligibility-criteria">5.1 Eligibility Criteria</h3>
      <p>To be eligible for leadership consideration, a volunteer must:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Be an active community member for at least 3 months.</li>
        <li>Participate in at least 5 community events or meetups.</li>
        <li>Contribute to community activities such as event organization, content delivery, social media posts, or mentorship.</li>
        <li>Demonstrate professionalism and collaboration.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>5.2 Evaluation Criteria</h3>
      <p>Candidates are evaluated based on:</p>
      <ol>
        <li>Community contribution.</li>
        <li>Technical expertise.</li>
        <li>Leadership potential.</li>
        <li>Communication and collaboration skills.</li>
        <li>Commitment to community values.</li>
        <li>Awareness of responsible and ethical AI practices.</li>
      </ol>

      <h3>5.3 Screening Process</h3>
      <p>The screening process follows these steps.</p>
      <ol>
        <li><strong>Nomination:</strong> Candidates may be nominated through leadership recommendation, self-nomination, or community member recommendation.</li>
        <li><strong>Leadership Review:</strong> The current leadership team reviews the candidate's contributions and engagement history.</li>
        <li><strong>Interview or Discussion:</strong> The candidate may participate in a short leadership discussion.</li>
        <li><strong>Decision:</strong> The leadership team decides whether to promote, retain, or re-evaluate later.</li>
      </ol>
    </section>

    <section id="recommendation-policy" class="policy-card">
      <h2>6. Community Recommendation Letter Policy</h2>
      <p>Recommendation letters from MMAUG are issued to support members and volunteers for:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Scholarships.</li>
        <li>Visa application recommendation letters.</li>
        <li>Professional opportunities.</li>
        <li>Community recognition.</li>
        <li>Academic programs.</li>
        <li>Industry conferences.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>Because these letters represent the reputation of the community, they are issued under strict criteria.</p>

      <h3>6.1 Eligibility Requirements</h3>
      <p>A member or volunteer may request a recommendation letter if they:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Have been an active member for at least 3 months.</li>
        <li>Have participated in multiple community events.</li>
        <li>Have contributed to community activities.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>Examples of acceptable contributions include delivering sessions, volunteering, contributing to projects, and mentoring.</p>

      <h3>6.2 Minimum Contribution Threshold</h3>
      <p>Recommendation letters are generally issued when a member has completed at least two of the following:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Delivered a technical session.</li>
        <li>Served as an event volunteer.</li>
        <li>Assisted in organizing community activities.</li>
        <li>Contributed educational content.</li>
        <li>Supported community initiatives.</li>
        <li>Demonstrated good behavioural and professional conduct in their workplace, with a reference letter from their employer.</li>
      </ul>

      <h3>6.3 Approval Process</h3>
      <ol>
        <li><strong>Formal request submission:</strong> Purpose, opportunity details, resume/profile, and employer reference letter.</li>
        <li><strong>Contribution review:</strong> Leadership reviews the member's contributions and engagement history.</li>
        <li><strong>Leadership approval:</strong> At least two leadership team members must approve the request.</li>
        <li><strong>Issuance:</strong> The letter is issued on behalf of the community.</li>
      </ol>
    </section>

    <section id="code-of-conduct-summary" class="policy-card">
      <h2>7. Code of Conduct</h2>
      <p>All members, volunteers, and leaders must adhere to the community's professional code of conduct.</p>
      <p>Participants are expected to:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Maintain respectful and inclusive interactions.</li>
        <li>Encourage collaboration and learning.</li>
        <li>Support diversity and inclusion.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>
        The community maintains zero tolerance for harassment, discrimination, or abusive behavior. Violations may lead
        to warning, removal from event participation, or suspension from community platforms.
      </p>
      <h3>Responsible AI Conduct</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Avoidance of misleading or exaggerated AI claims.</li>
        <li>Respect for data privacy, fairness, and inclusivity in AI discussions and demonstrations.</li>
      </ul>
      <p><a href="{{ '/code-of-conduct/' | relative_url }}">Read the full Code of Conduct</a></p>
    </section>

    <section id="event-process" class="policy-card">
      <h2>8. Community Event Process</h2>
      <p>Events follow a structured lifecycle.</p>
      <h3>Planning Phase</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Identify topics.</li>
        <li>Confirm speakers.</li>
        <li>Secure venue where applicable for in-person events.</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Promotion Phase</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Publish the event on Meetup.</li>
        <li>Promote through social channels.</li>
        <li>Notify community members.</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Execution Phase</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Event registration.</li>
        <li>Session delivery.</li>
        <li>Networking.</li>
      </ul>
      <h3>Post-Event Phase</h3>
      <ul>
        <li>Share session recordings.</li>
        <li>Collect feedback.</li>
        <li>Recognize speakers and volunteers.</li>
      </ul>
    </section>

    <section id="growth-strategy" class="policy-card">
      <h2>9. Community Growth Strategy</h2>
      <p>The Malta Microsoft AI User Group will grow through:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>High-quality technical content.</li>
        <li>Collaboration with Microsoft and industry partners.</li>
        <li>Continuous community engagement.</li>

    <section id="ai-governance" class="policy-card">
      <h2>10. AI Governance and Continual Improvement</h2>

      <h3>10.1 Purpose</h3>
      <p>
        MMAUG recognises that the responsible discussion, demonstration, and promotion of artificial intelligence
        technologies requires ongoing governance, reflection, and improvement.
      </p>
      <p>
        This section defines how AI-related governance practices are reviewed, improved, and adapted over time to ensure
        continued alignment with ethical, professional, and community values.
      </p>

      <h3>10.2 Review of AI-Related Feedback and Incidents</h3>
      <p>The community maintains a structured but lightweight approach to reviewing AI-related feedback and concerns.</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Feedback from members, attendees, speakers, or partners relating to ethical concerns, misleading AI claims, data privacy, or fairness issues.</li>
        <li>Reported incidents where AI-related content or activities may have caused confusion or harm, breached the Code of Conduct, or created reputational or ethical risk.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>All such feedback or incidents will be logged, reviewed where appropriate, and assessed proportionately.</p>

      <h3>10.3 Updating Governance Policies and Processes</h3>
      <p>Where reviews identify gaps, risks, or opportunities for improvement, the leadership team may:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Update governance policies, guidance, or processes.</li>
        <li>Refine event planning or speaker engagement practices.</li>
        <li>Introduce additional awareness or guidance for volunteers or leaders.</li>
      </ul>
      <p>Changes will be proportionate, clearly communicated, and documented for transparency and continuity.</p>

      <h3 id="continual-improvement">10.4 Continual Improvement Approach</h3>
      <p>MMAUG is committed to continual improvement of its AI governance practices through:</p>
      <ul>
        <li>Periodic leadership reviews that include AI governance effectiveness.</li>
        <li>Reflection on lessons learned from events and activities.</li>
        <li>Alignment with evolving best practices in responsible and ethical AI.</li>
      </ul>
